The NTU’s Laboratory of Intelligent Materials and Systems investigates emerging non-volatile memory and sensory materials. Specifically, strong interest is given to the development of intelligent technology using non-volatile memory devices. The research team conducts simulations for device and circuit designs, and develops devices using advanced micro- and nano-fabrication techniques. Our laboratory equipped with the state-of-the-art micro- and nano-fabrication, film deposition, and characterisation facilities. 

Our research interest extends to the study and development of micro- and nano-electromechanical devices for magnetic sensing in biological and biomedical applications. We collaborate closely with industry for solution of technology, and with academic institutions for fundamental research.
Our group focuses on magnetism and magnetic materials research, both on experimental and theoretical investigations. Specifically, we have strong interest in developing spintronic devices for memory, logic, and sensor applications.

1. Magnetic domain wall devices

2. Magnetic logic devices

3. Spin orbit torque

4. Magnetoresistive sensors

5. Magneto-fluidics devices

6. Magnetic nanoparticles

7. Skyrmions dynamics

9. Resistive random access memory (RRAM)
